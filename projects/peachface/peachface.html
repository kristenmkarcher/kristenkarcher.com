    Peachface Cosmetics is a website specializing in essential makeup products to make everyone feel and look their best.

    What is Peachface Cosmetics?

    Peachface Cosmetics is a website that introduces users to high-quality makeup products. I designed this site to be easy to navigate, with the most sought after products readily accesible for purchase.

    The idea for Peachface was inspired from various frustrations I have had navigating other retail websites, and of course, my love of cosmetics.

    What is Peachface Cosmetics trying to solve?

    Shopping online should be a stress-free and enjoyable experience for the user. I designed Peachface to be simple to navigate for anyone to use; from the basic user to the computer saavy.

    User Surveys

    I wanted to create a user survey to discover more about who would be using my website. My goals were to find out their purchasing habits. For example, do they prefer to buy online or in-store? How much do they spend on cosmetics monthly?

    Through email and social media, my survey was completed by 20 different respondents. Below are my findings.

    80% prefer to buy online versus in a store


    45% spend more than $40 monthly on cosmetics


    55% test items in stores, but purchase online


    25% prefer to shop from their phones


    40% prefer guest checkout versus creating an account

    User Personas

    To get a better understanding of my users and what expectations they have for a retail website, I decided to give a more in-depth interview to three different users. Finding out what goals and frustrations they may have while shopping was very beneficial while designing my site.

    Katherine

    Student

    Age: 21 | Location: Stratford, CT | Gender: Female

    "As a busy student, the last thing I want to worry about is my makeup disappearing by the end of the day."

    Goal

    Ensuring the user that the site only stocks quality products that will keep them feeling worry-free and confident from morning to night.

    Frustration

    Going from class to class, Katherine has a difficult time finding quality cosmetic products that will keep her looking fresh all day.

    Bio

    Katherine is currently a student studying Communication Disorders at Southern Connecticut State University. While not in class, you can find her in the Office of Graduate Admissions as a student worker, where she helps perspective graduate students with the application process. In her free time, Katherine likes to read, volunteer and go shopping.

    Lauren

    Recreation Supervisor

    Age: 56 | Location: Oxford, CT | Gender: Female

    "Sometimes trustworthy reviews are hard to find."

    Goal

    Giving the user the option to leave a review of a product and the ability to read reviews left by other users, including users who are frequent "verified" customers.

    Frustration

    Since cosmetics can get expensive, Lauren likes to read reviews for products she has never tried before.

    Bio

    Lauren is a married mom of two girls, currently employed at a local elementary school as a Recreation Supervisor. Keeping the children safe during their free play time is the main objective of this position. Outside of school, Lauren enjoys cooking, the daily crossword puzzle and family vacations to Maine.

    Competitive Analysis

    I wanted to analyze a few top cosmetic brands based on affordability and how they cater to their customers in terms of ease of purchasing items. While all three brands offered “quick shop” pop-up windows to quickly select a product to add to cart, I noticed Urban Decay and Cle de Peau both offered an option for the user to auto-replenish their order in the future. Urban Decay also offers the user to checkout with Shoprunner, which guarantees free shipping and returns, as well as 2-day delivery. Each of the websites also allow the user to sign in using a social media account instead of creating an account or checking out as a guest.

    Affordable

    How site allows users to filter products

    The ability to filter products by price, finish and formula is located through vertical left navigation. There is also a search bar provided for users to search for a specific item.

    How site allows users to select category pages

    All products are categorized through a horizontal nav bar at the top of the home page.

    How site allows users to select items to purchase

    Products can be selected to purchase through a “quick shop” pop-up window, or by clicking the name of the item. There are drop down menus to select both color and quantity.

    Checkout process for purchasing items

    Users can checkout securely by signing in as a registered user, through social media (Google+, Twitter, Instagram, Facebook) or as a guest. Purchases can be made with PayPal or credit cards, and there is also an area for promotional codes.

    High-end

    How site allows users to filter products

    Products can be filtered by color, price, favorites and best sellers. Users also have the ability to search for a specific item.

    How site allows users to select category pages

    All products are categorized through a vertical drop down at the top of the home page.

    How site allows users to select items to purchase

    Products can be selected to purchase through a “quick shop” pop-up window, or by clicking the name of the item. There are drop down menus to select both color and quantity, as well as an option to deliver one-time only or auto-replenish your order.

    Checkout process for purchasing items

    Users can checkout securely by signing in as a registered user, through social media (Google+, Twitter, Instagram, Facebook) or as a guest. Purchases can be made with PayPal or credit cards, and there is also an area for promotional codes. This site also offers an option to checkout with Shoprunner, which guarantees free shipping/returns and 2-day delivery.

    How site allows users to filter products

    Products can be filtered by formula, finish, best sellers. and new arrivals. A search bar is also located at the top right of the page.

    How site allows users to select category pages

    All products are categorized through horizontal navigation and vertical drop down menus at the top of the home page.

    How site allows users to select items to purchase

    Products can be selected to purchase through a “quick shop” pop-up window, or by clicking the name of the item. There are drop down menus to select both color and quantity, as well as an option to deliver one-time only or auto-replenish your order after a specified number of weeks available through a drop down box.

    Designing the Product Detail Pages

    The product detail pages were designed to be as easy as possible for the user to choose an item to purchase and add to their cart. Users are provided with reviews and the option to leave a review, the option to choose a certain quantity of the item, a list of both product details and ingredients, and the option to choose different colors of the item.

    Designing the Shopping Cart

    The shopping cart was designed for even the most basic user to have an easy, positive shopping experience. The checkout process is made up of 3 steps after the user confirms their order. A list of the items they are purchasing, including a photo of the product, quantity and price is included in the cart, as well as an order summary and how the user can contact customer service if need be. There is also a section to view information on the shipping and return policy.

    Designing the Home Page

    I attempted to create a home page with large call outs drawing the users attention to the main product categories. Through the use of large, bold images I hope to capture the users attention as soon as they enter the site.

    Preference Test for High-fidelity Wireframes

    Through wireframe testing, final options came down to a larger navigation section versus a more streamlined design. Ultimately, option A won out, as this allowed the main hero image to gain more attention from the user. The call to action buttonswere also placed more towards the bottom of the images to put more of an emphasis on each hero image.

    Preference Test for Final Design

    After creating a preference test for the final design, it was decided option B would ultimately be the choice. The pop of blue from the water in the image on option B was a nice contrast to the rest of the colors on the page. The pink bars on the top navigation were also made smaller, and the background color on the navigation was made a lighter shade of pink to make each feature stand out a bit more.

    What I learned Throughout the Process

    As this was the first project I designed on my own, there were many things I learned going through this process. Through the design process, I greatly increased my abilities with Sketch and the Adobe suite. Researching user feedback also provided many benefits through the design process. While I initially had many conceptions as to what made a great site, the insight gained through user surveys and personas allowed me to make beneficial changes to my overall design.
