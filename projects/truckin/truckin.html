"Driving home the goodness"

Truckin' Delicious is a mobile app where you can place orders, pay and pick up food from your favorite local truck.

What is Truckin' Delicious?

Truckin' Delicious is a mobile app that allows users to search, order, and purchase from food trucks in their area. Users can discover new dining options hidden in their areas. Truckin' Delicious gives users a chance to connect with smaller locally owned food trucks.

The idea for Truckin' Delicious came from observing my local food truck scene. Everyday, dozens of trucks gather in a nearby town with little advertising, other than social media and word of mouth. I invision Truckin' Delicious to be a way for businesses like these to advertise to a larger audience.

What is Truckin' Delicious trying to solve?

Truckin' Delicious is trying to solve the issue of "what do I eat today?" Too often, people rely on the same food choices, rarely being exposed to the new and delicious options in their area. Truckin' Delicious allows users to search a variety of local cuisines that may be hidden right under their noses.

User Surveys

I wanted to create a user survey to discover more about who would be using my app. My goals were to find out how people were choosing their dining options and what would it take for them to try something new.

Through email and social media, my survey was completed by 26 different respondents. Below are my findings.

62% of users order out at least once per week


86% of users would prefer to place their order and pay in advance


53% of users like to try new restaurants each time they eat out


74% of users are unaware of all the dining options within a 5 block radius of their location


67% of users rely on reviews when trying new restaurants

Competitive Analysis

I wanted to analyze a few top food apps based on how they allow users to search for meal options and pay for them. I chose to compare Eat24 and Grubhub while developing my concept. Eat24 and Grubhub both allow the user to search for food by specific item, cuisine, restaurant, price and if they offer delivery. While selecting items to purchase, they offer detailed descriptions of each item and the option to add any "special instructions" to your order. For example, food allergies or "hold the onions". The checkout process is also similar for both apps. The user has the ability to select delivery or pickup, shows the delivery charge upfront, and gives the option to select an amount for tip. While they have many similarities, Eat24 and Grubhub differ in that Eat24 allows the user to view the profile and full menu of a restaurant, even if they are closed, and Grubhub does not.

How site allows users to filter products

Users can either search for a specific food item or filter by cuisine, restaurant, distance, price, delivery charge and who offers coupons.

How site allows users to select profile pages

When looking at restaurants in your area, the app will notify the user if the restaurant is closed, but will still allow the user to look at the entire profile page.

How site allows users to select items to purchase

When an item is selected, a new screen will appear allowing the user to select quantity, special instructions, as well as a more detailed description of the item.

Checkout process for purchasing items

The checkout process allows the user to select delivery/pickup, shows the delivery charge upfront, and gives an option to view the menu again.

Logo Design

The logo for Truckin' Delicious is inspired by the location pin on a map. My first experience with a food truck was going to a series of Mexican trucks in my neighboring city. Creating the pin with a taco serves as a reminder of this experience, along with symbolizing one of the most iconic food truck cuisines.

My choice of light green and blue again goes back to my first food truck experience.The first trucks I visited are lined up by the ocean, and these colors remind me of that location I still so often. I used the bright pink color as a callout to draw attention to small features in the app. The font I used for my logo reminded me of the ocean waves and brings about a feeling of summertime and eating a delicious lunch outside.

User flow using Balsamiq

It was important to create a user flow to get a high-level look at the app before each wireframe was put together.

High-fidelity Wireframes

Designing the Product Detail Pages

The product detail pages were designed to be as easy as possible for the user to choose an item to purchase and add to their cart. The user has the option to add or remove the quantity of an item, add additional options to their order, including extra toppings and sides, and add any special instructions to their order, such food allergies or additions to meals.

Designing the Shopping Cart

The shopping cart was designed for even the most basic user to easily and quickly order their meal. The checkout process starts with a summary of the full order, and allows the user to remove any items one final time before purchase. The payment screen gives several different options to pay, including Apple Pay and PayPal, along with the ability for the user to leave their own specific amount for tip, or to select from a default amount.

Designing the Home Page

I attempted to create a home page that displays a list of the trucks located closest to the user. I prominently displayed a few details about each truck, including location, cuisine, reviews, distance and price. By tapping on a truck, the user will be brought to that specific trucks profile page.

Preference Test for High-fidelity Wireframes

Through wireframe testing, final options came down to how I organized the layout of the details of each truck. Ultimately, option B won out, because of the more natural and appealing layout of the elements on each row.

Preference Test for Final Design

After creating a preference test for the final design, it was decided option B would ultimately be the choice. While I liked the pink for the heading text, it did not test well with users. The green and blue is easier on the eyes, while still calling attention to each section.

What I learned Throughout the Process

Truckin' Delicious allowed me to learn how to design specifically for a mobile application. Through this process, I learned how some design choices made on a desktop application do not work out as well on mobile devices. I had to focus on mobile usability, and not just the design of the app. This project taught me how to emphasize each truck with a limited amount of space, due to the size limitations of the device. This process was challenging, yet very fun and I enjoyed creating this app.
